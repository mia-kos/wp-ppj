Check you are eligible

You must:

  • Have a right to work in the UK
  • Be a UK resident for at least 3 years
  • Be 18 years old or over
  • Not be a member of any group of organisation that does not fit with the prison service values of diversity and inclusion

Being a prison officer takes quick thinking, accuracy and the ability to handle intense situations. We test the following through online questionnaires and assessment days.

Eyesight

Can you read a car number plate from 10 metres away without wearing glasses or contact lenses?

Role play

Test yourself to see if you have the skills and characteristics to be a prison officer

Fitness

You will need a reasonable level of stamina, strength and fitness to show you can be on your feet all day and handle emergencies.

Numeracy

Can you complete basic maths questions to show you can do things like a prisoner headcount?

You will be sent 15 multiple choice questions on numeracy and how you would react to certain situations you might face as a prison officer. We use these to check you have the basic qualities to be a prison officer.

We will let you know if you have passed the online test within one day. If you are successful, we invite you to attend a recruitment assessment day (RAD).

We also operate a guaranteed interview scheme for disabled people who meet the minimum criteria for appointment (as defined by the Disability Discrimination Act 1995)

You will be invited to visit the prison where you have applied for a job. This is a great chance to meet serving prison officers and see what it’s like to work in the prison. Organising a visit can take up to 3 weeks.

We will assess whether you have what it takes to be a successful prison officer using a mix of individual, group and online tests. We will give you guidance beforehand on what to expect on the day.

Role play - three 10-minute simulations you could face as a prison officer.

Written test - read and answer questions about different scenarios which demonstrate your maths and written English.

Written test - an eye test, basic health screening, including blood pressure check, and a fitness test of your strength and agility to show you can handle the physical demands of the job.

If you pass the assessment day, we carry out some initial background checks and you will immediately be offered a conditional employment contract.

What you will need

  • Proof of identity, such a passport, driving licence or birth certificate
  • Proof of address, such as a utility bill or bank statement from the last 3 months
  • Job references for the last 5 years, including for any employment gaps
  • Gym clothes and trainers (in a clear plastic bag if the assessment is in a prison)
  • Lunch and snacks
  • Comfortable shoes as you will on your feet throughout the day

If the prison officer position is available immediately, we carry out background checks for security and identity before you begin the job. This can take up to 12 weeks.

If a position is not available, you will be put onto a reserve list. We only begin the background checks when there is a vacancy for you.
